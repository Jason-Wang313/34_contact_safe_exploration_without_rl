# Claims

Claim 1: Contact exploration should treat probe safety as the primary state variable, not as a penalty learned after unsafe actions.

Claim 2: A certificate-expansion policy can cover more useful contact cells than a conservative policy while causing far fewer violations than random force exploration.

Claim 3: In the deterministic toy benchmark, certificate expansion covers 26.393 cells on average with 1240 total violations, compared with random force exploration at 98684 violations and conservative exploration at 14.710 covered cells.
